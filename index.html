In Brief

A Wanderer Between Disciplines

I am a graduate student in Physics at Técnico Lisboa, currently wrapping up both my BSc and my MS in Engineering Physics. Albeit unconventional, my academic journey blends hands-on laboratory work, computational analysis and simulation, and theoretical research.

I'm currently working at the intersection of nuclear physics and network science, to investigate how knowledge diffuses through scholarly networks. My work concerns how knowledge is made, transmitted, and transformed, particulary in the context of women in science and their struggle to be recognized as full members of the scientific community.

Alongside my research, I write code as a freelance web developer, read a lot of books on a variety of subjects, travel way too little to my heart's content, and spend a perhaps unreasonable amount of time thinking about medieval manuscripts and people who died centuries ago. I have a soft spot for libraries, archives, and all things old and dusty, and I am fascinated by the history of science and the evolution of scientific ideas.
