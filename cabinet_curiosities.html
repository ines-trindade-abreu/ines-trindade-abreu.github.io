What Lives Here

This cabinet holds the curious, the unfinished, and the deeply interesting. My research sits at the boundary between the exact sciences and the humanities — between the nucleus of an atom and the network of people who first named it, measured it, and argued over what it meant.

At Técnico Lisboa I have built a foundation in nuclear and radiation physics, with a particular interest in their applications to health and environmental sciences. In parallel, I have found myself increasingly drawn to the history and sociology of science: how knowledge is produced, who gets credit, and how ideas travel through communities of scholars across time.

My MSc thesis brings these two worlds together, applying network analysis to the early radioactivity community — a moment in history when the discipline was being invented, the rules were still being written, and some of the most important contributors were women navigating a deeply unequal field.
